to either (1) utilize hormonal contraception plus one barrier method or (2) use two barrier methods of
contraception (e.g. diaphragm and spermicide) from initial screening until one month after taking the final
dose. An intrauterine device (IUD) is considered a barrier method of contraception in this study. Male subjects
must be willing to inform female partners of their participation in the study and must agree to use adequate
contraceptive methods (vasectomy performed at least 6 months prior to first study drug administration, or use
at least one barrier method of birth control).~6. Able to understand and comply with the study procedures,
voluntarily agree to participate in this study, and provide written informed consent prior to start of any
study-specific procedures.~7. All subjects must have a caregiver who is willing and able to ensure compliance
with study medications, visits, and study procedures.~
